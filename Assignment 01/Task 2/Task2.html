BLACK HOLE

A black hole is a place in space where gravity pulls so much that even light can not get out. The gravity is so strong because matter has been squeezed into a tiny space. This can happen when a star is dying. Because no light can get out, people can't see black hole. They are invisible. Space telescopes with special tools can help find black holes. The special tools can see how stars that are very close to black holes act differently than other stars.

Could a Black Hole Destroy Earth?
Black holes do not go around in space eating stars, moons and planets. Earth will not fall into a black holes because no black holes is close enough to the solar system for Earth to do that. Even if a black holes the same mass as the sun were to take the place of the sun, Earth still would not fall in. The black holes would have the same gravity as the sun. Earth and the other planets would orbit the black holes as they orbit the sun now. The sun will never turn into a black holes. The sun is not a big enough star to make a black holes.
How Is NASA Studying black holes?
NASA is using satellites and telescopes that are traveling in space to learn more about black holes. These spacecraft help scientists answer questions about the universe.
